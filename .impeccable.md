## Design Context

### Users
Voyage is for frequent premium travelers who want a composed trip companion while they are preparing for a journey or already moving through it. They use the app in real travel contexts: packing the night before, checking details in transit, and grounding themselves once they arrive.

### Brand Personality
Composed, editorial, concierge-like. The interface should feel calm under pressure, exact without being cold, and premium without becoming flashy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Preserve Voyage's existing ink-and-parchment travel language, serif-led trip storytelling, and tactile aviation details. The active trip companion should feel like a refined travel folio rather than a generic media player: dark transport surfaces, warm paper sections, restrained motion, and Apple-native interaction patterns. Anti-reference: glossy glassmorphism, neon travel dashboards, and dense airline-operations UI.

### Design Principles
1. Keep the trip visible without making the app feel crowded; the mini-player should feel persistent and calm.
2. Use hierarchy through scale, contrast, and spacing instead of stacking generic cards inside cards.
3. Treat motion as orientation, not decoration; transitions should clarify state changes and preserve control.
4. Make operational actions feel trustworthy: GPS controls, day navigation, and nearby actions should be obvious, roomy, and reversible.
5. Preserve a premium travel tone across empty and scaffolded states so partial data still feels intentional.
